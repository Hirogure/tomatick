## Introduction to Tomatick

Tomatick is a free, beautiful online Pomodoro timer built to help you focus. Unlike heavy productivity suites with endless features, Tomatick does one thing exceptionally well: it helps you get into a focused work rhythm using the proven Pomodoro Technique.

No sign-up required. No downloads. Just open the app and start working.

In this guide, we'll walk you through everything — from starting your very first Pomodoro session to mastering the app's features.

## Setting Up Your First Pomodoro Session

Getting started with Tomatick takes less than 60 seconds.

**Step 1: Open the app.** Navigate to [tomatick.app](https://tomatick.app) in any modern browser. You'll see the timer display showing 25:00, ready to start.

**Step 2: Add a task.** In the task input area below the timer, type what you want to work on. For example: "Write project proposal introduction." Press Enter or click Add to create the task.

**Step 3: Select your task.** Click on the task you just added to mark it as the active task. This helps you stay focused on exactly one thing.

**Step 4: Start the timer.** Press the **Space bar** or click the **Start** button. The timer begins counting down from 25:00.

**Step 5: Work.** Focus entirely on your chosen task until the timer rings. Resist the urge to check social media, respond to non-urgent messages, or do anything else.

**Step 6: Take your break.** When the alarm sounds, you've completed one Pomodoro. Take a 5-minute break — stand up, stretch, get water. Tomatick will automatically switch to a break timer.

**Step 7: Repeat.** After four Pomodoros, you'll earn a 15–30 minute long break. Tomatick tracks this for you automatically.

## Using the Task Manager

The task manager is one of Tomatick's most useful features. Here's how to use it effectively:

**Adding tasks:** Type your task in the input field at the bottom of the task list and press Enter. Keep task names specific and action-oriented: "Draft email to client" rather than "email."

**Setting the active task:** Click any task to make it active. The active task is highlighted so you always know what you're supposed to be working on.

**Completing tasks:** When a task is done, check it off. Completed tasks are visually marked so you can see your day's progress at a glance.

**Managing your list:** Keep your task list focused. Aim for 3–5 tasks per day. Large projects should be broken down into small, Pomodoro-sized chunks.

**Pro tip:** Before starting your workday, spend one Pomodoro on planning. Write out the tasks you want to complete and estimate how many Pomodoros each will take. This simple practice dramatically improves daily output.

## Keyboard Shortcuts

Tomatick is designed to keep you in flow without requiring the mouse. Here are the key shortcuts:

- **Space** — Start or pause the current timer
- The timer automatically transitions between work and break modes

Using the Space bar to control the timer means you never have to leave the keyboard during a Pomodoro session.

## Understanding Session Tracking

Tomatick automatically tracks your Pomodoro sessions throughout the day. At the top of the timer, you can see which session you're on (e.g., Pomodoro 1 of 4 before your next long break).

**Session counter:** The small indicators near the timer show how many Pomodoros you've completed in the current cycle. After four, you'll automatically be prompted to take a long break.

**Daily tracking:** Your completed sessions and task completions are saved locally in your browser. This means your data stays private — it never leaves your device.

**Why tracking matters:** Knowing your daily Pomodoro count is surprisingly motivating. On days you complete 8–10 Pomodoros, you'll notice significantly more progress on your important work. Use the count as a personal KPI.

## Customizing Your Settings

Tomatick lets you adjust the timer to fit your work style. Click the **⚙ settings icon** on the timer to access preferences:

**Focus duration:** Change from the default 25 minutes. Many knowledge workers prefer 50-minute sessions; students often stick with 25.

**Short break duration:** Default is 5 minutes. Increase to 10 if you find 5 minutes too rushed.

**Long break duration:** Default is 15 minutes. A 20–30 minute break can be more restorative after a demanding work cycle.

**Sound notifications:** Toggle the alarm sound on or off. The audio cue is important for deep work — when you're truly focused, you don't want to be watching the timer.

**Theme:** Tomatick supports light and dark modes to match your environment and preference.

## Getting the Most Out of Tomatick

Here are a few final tips to maximize your results with Tomatick:

**Use it every day.** The Pomodoro Technique becomes more powerful as a habit. The more consistently you use it, the better you'll get at estimating tasks and protecting your focus time.
